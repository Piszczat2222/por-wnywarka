## Why Roomba Still Owns the Category

iRobot invented the robot vacuum category and the Roomba name is basically a verb. The j7 series adds smart obstacle detection (hello, charging cables), strong carpet performance, and a polished app experience. If you have pets, kids, and mixed flooring, it's a proven workhorse that dodges socks better than most competitors.

You're also paying **$400–500** for that pedigree, sometimes more with a self-empty base. Roomba's PrecisionVision and years of software refinement matter in cluttered homes where cheaper robots get stuck weekly.

## How We Compared These Two

We compared both robots on **navigation**, **suction**, **noise**, **convenience**, and **value for apartments**. We reviewed eufy 11S MAX's 58,000+ Amazon ratings, checked pet-hair feedback, and focused on single-level homes with hard floors and medium-pile carpets where budget robots shine.

Comparison highlights:

- **Obstacle avoidance**: Roomba j7 leads on cables and small objects; 11S MAX uses simpler bump navigation
- **Suction**: Roomba stronger on thick carpet; eufy BoostIQ handles apartments and medium-pile well
- **Profile**: 11S MAX is super-thin and fits under more furniture
- **Features**: Roomba offers mapping and premium app; 11S MAX focuses on vacuuming basics done well
- **Price**: roughly $450 vs $160, saving nearly $300

## Why eufy 11S MAX Wins on Value

**eufy 11S MAX** is Amazon's **Overall Pick** with **58,000+ reviews** and 6k+ monthly purchases. It's super-thin, quiet, self-charging, and often lands around **$160** on sale. For apartments and single-level homes with hard floors and medium-pile rugs, reviewers report it handles daily maintenance impressively well.

It won't map your rooms or dodge every sock like a j7. What it will do is run on a schedule, tuck under couches, and keep pet hair off your floors without a $400 commitment. For first-time robot buyers, that's the right entry point.

## What You Give Up (and What You Keep)

You give up Roomba's best-in-class obstacle AI, LiDAR room mapping, mopping, self-empty bases, and the most refined app polish. Cluttered floors with cables and toys still challenge basic navigation.

What you keep is **daily pet hair pickup**, **quiet operation**, **self-charging convenience**, and a **super-thin profile** that reaches more dust bunnies. For first-time robot buyers testing whether they'll actually use the thing, the lower price removes commitment anxiety.

## The Bottom Line

Cluttered multi-room homes with kids, pets, and cables everywhere should buy Roomba j7+. Apartment dwellers and budget pet owners who want set-and-forget daily cleaning on hard floors should buy eufy 11S MAX. Let the robot handle maintenance; you handle the savings.
