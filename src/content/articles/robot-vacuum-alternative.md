## Why Roomba Still Owns the Category

iRobot invented the robot vacuum category and the Roomba name is basically a verb. The j7 series adds smart obstacle detection (hello, charging cables), strong carpet performance, and a polished app experience. If you have pets, kids, and mixed flooring, it's a proven workhorse.

You're also paying **$400–500** for that pedigree — sometimes more with a self-empty base.

## Why eufy RoboVac L35 Hybrid+ Wins on Value

**eufy** (by Anker) has quietly become Amazon's robot vacuum powerhouse. The **RoboVac L35 Hybrid+** combines LiDAR room mapping, vacuum-and-mop functionality, and an auto-empty station — often for **under $200** on sale.

For apartments and single-level homes, reviewers report it handles daily maintenance impressively well. It won't dodge every sock like a j7, but the 60% savings buys a lot of tolerance.

### Who this is for

- Apartment dwellers with hard floors and area rugs
- Pet owners on a budget who want daily hair pickup
- First-time robot vacuum buyers testing the waters

### Who should skip

- Large multi-story homes without carry support
- Homes with tons of floor clutter (cables, toys)
- You want the absolute best obstacle AI

### Pros

- Hybrid mop + vacuum at budget price
- LiDAR mapping with room zones in app
- Auto-empty reduces manual dumping
- Anker/eufy ecosystem reliability

### Cons

- Obstacle avoidance less advanced than Roomba j7
- Mop is maintenance-light, not deep scrub
- Customer service can be slower than iRobot
