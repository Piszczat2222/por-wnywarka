## Why the Ninja Creami Took Over TikTok

The Ninja Creami turned **protein ice cream** and one-ingredient banana soft serve into a mainstream kitchen flex. Freeze a pint, spin it, re-spin for texture — the workflow is uniquely satisfying. At ~$200, it's a serious appliance purchase for a single use case.

## Why Cuisinart Is Our Budget Pick

The Cuisinart ICE-21 (and similar models) uses the **classic freezer-bowl churn** method. You pre-freeze the bowl, pour in base, and churn for 15–25 minutes. Results are closer to traditional homemade ice cream than Creami's pint-spin texture — but for fruit sorbets, frozen yogurt, and standard ice cream bases, it's excellent.

You won't get the exact **"Creamify" re-spin workflow** for rock-hard frozen pints. If your goal is general frozen desserts and weekend treats — not daily macro-friendly protein bowls — this saves ~$130.

## Who Should Still Buy the Ninja Creami?

Dedicated fitness creators, people who eat protein ice cream **daily**, and anyone who wants the specific pint-freeze-re-spin ritual. For occasional homemade desserts, Cuisinart is the smarter budget move.
