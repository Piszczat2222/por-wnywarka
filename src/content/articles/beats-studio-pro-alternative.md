## Why Beats Studio Pro Still Command the Price

**Beats Studio Pro** is Apple's flagship over-ear: USB-C lossless audio, spatial audio, and ANC tuned for iPhone users who want the Beats look in the studio and on flights. At **$320–380**, you're paying for brand, ecosystem integration, and polish that shows in the padding, hinge, and carrying case. The bass-forward Beats signature is iconic, and Apple Music lossless over USB-C is a genuine perk for subscribers.

For Android users or anyone who doesn't need Apple Music lossless, the value proposition thins fast. But iPhone commuters who want one pair of over-ears that do everything Apple offers will find the Studio Pro hard to replace within Apple's own lineup.

## How We Compared These Two

We evaluated **ANC quality**, **sound tuning**, **battery life**, **comfort for long sessions**, and **ecosystem value**. We also referenced our full [AirPods Max alternative](/articles/airpods-max-alternative) testing of the Soundcore Space One, since both comparisons point to the same Amazon pick for over-ear value.

- **ANC performance**: Beats lead on commute tuning; Soundcore handles offices and coffee shops well
- **Audio codecs**: Beats offer USB-C lossless with Apple Music; Soundcore offers LDAC for Android and iPhone
- **Battery**: both claim up to 40 hours depending on ANC settings
- **Build feel**: Beats premium padding and foldable design vs Soundcore's lighter plastic build
- **Price**: roughly $350 vs $99, saving over $250

## Why Soundcore Space One Is Our Pick

We already featured **Soundcore Space One** in our AirPods Max alternative review, and they remain one of Amazon's best over-ear values. Hybrid ANC, LDAC support, 40-hour battery, and a foldable design under $100 make them the obvious **Beats Studio Pro alternative** for budget shoppers.

You won't get Beats branding, USB-C lossless through Apple's preferred path, or spatial audio tricks. You will get comfortable daily headphones that don't require a credit card limit increase, plus an app for EQ tuning that lets you push bass toward Beats territory if you want.

## What You Give Up (and What You Keep)

You give up Apple's lossless USB-C pipeline, spatial audio, premium materials, and the social signal of Beats on your head in a coffee shop. ANC on airplanes isn't class-leading, and the plastic build doesn't feel as refined when you handle both side by side.

What you keep is **real over-ear ANC**, long battery life, LDAC hi-res audio, multipoint Bluetooth, and a comfortable fit for multi-hour work sessions. For open offices, commuting, and studying, the daily experience is strong enough that the $250+ savings are hard to ignore.

Apple ecosystem power users who want USB-C lossless and spatial audio should buy Beats Studio Pro.
