## Why Theragun Owns the Recovery Category

**Theragun Pro** is what physios and pro athletes show on Instagram — triangular grip, deep percussion, app-guided routines, and a price that screams serious recovery. At **$450–550**, you're buying Therabody's R&D and brand trust as much as the motor.

For chronic pain sufferers and high-volume athletes, that can be worth it.

## Why Bob and Brad D6 Pro Is Our Pick

**Bob and Brad** massage guns dominate Amazon's recovery category — designed by physical therapists (the brand name), with amplitude and speed settings that satisfy most post-leg-day soreness. The **D6 Pro** tier hits the sweet spot under $100 with multiple heads and a case.

You won't impress anyone at the NFL training room. You will loosen tight quads and traps after squats without financing a wellness gadget.

### Who this is for

- Gym regulars with DOMS who can't justify Theragun
- Runners and desk workers with tight shoulders
- Gift buyers wanting useful fitness gear under $100

### Who should skip

- Physical therapists buying for clinic daily use
- You need the quietest possible motor in shared spaces
- Chronic pain requiring medical-grade guidance

### Pros

- Deep percussion for the price
- Long battery on many models
- Multiple attachment heads
- PT-founded brand with solid reviews

### Cons

- Louder than Theragun at max speed
- Plastic build less premium
- App and smart features limited vs Therabody
