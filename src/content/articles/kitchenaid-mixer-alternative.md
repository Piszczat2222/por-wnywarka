## Why KitchenAid Is the Dream Mixer

The KitchenAid Artisan is the mixer in every Pinterest kitchen. The weight, the color options, the attachment ecosystem (pasta roller, ice cream bowl, grain mill) — it's a machine you buy once and keep for decades. Home bakers treat it like furniture.

That longevity comes at **$300–400** new, and trendy colors can cost even more.

## Why Hamilton Beach Stand Mixer Delivers

The **Hamilton Beach Stand Mixer** hits the sweet spot for casual and intermediate bakers. Five-quart capacity, tilt-head access, and enough power for cookies, cakes, and most bread doughs — typically around **$120**.

It won't match KitchenAid's motor on stiff bagel dough every day, and the attachment aftermarket isn't as vast. But if your goal is weekend baking and aesthetic counter presence, it's the pick most budget guides recommend.

### Who this is for

- New bakers furnishing a first kitchen
- Anyone who bakes monthly, not daily
- Decor-focused cooks who want the stand mixer look

### Who should skip

- Serious bread bakers running multiple batches weekly
- You plan to invest in pasta/grain attachments
- Commercial-level durability requirements

### Pros

- Real 5qt capacity for family recipes
- Tilt-head ease of use
- Fraction of KitchenAid cost
- Solid Amazon review history

### Cons

- Motor can strain on very stiff dough
- Lighter base — may walk on high speed
- Fewer color and attachment options
