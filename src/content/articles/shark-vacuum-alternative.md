## Why Shark Stratos Is a Step Below Dyson, Above Budget

**Shark Stratos** cordless vacuums sit in the sweet spot for many American homes — better than cheap stick vacs, cheaper than Dyson, with **PowerFins** anti-hair-wrap and a lift-away pod for stairs. At **$300–400**, Shark earned loyalty from pet owners who don't want to finance cleaning equipment.

Still, that's a meaningful chunk of change for a tool you use twice a week.

## Why Eureka RapidClean Pro Is Our Amazon Pick

If Shark pricing stings, **Eureka RapidClean Pro** delivers cordless convenience and respectable suction for roughly **half the Shark Stratos bill**. It's the same pick we recommend in our Dyson vacuum comparison — not because it's identical to Shark, but because it solves the same job for budget buyers.

You lose Shark's anti-tangle engineering and brand support network. You gain cash for actually hiring a cleaner once in a while.

### Who this is for

- Shark-curious shoppers who can't stretch to Stratos
- Pet owners in smaller homes and apartments
- Anyone replacing an old plug-in upright

### Who should skip

- Large homes with thick carpet throughout
- You specifically want Shark's hair-wrap tech
- Warranty and US customer service are top priority

### Pros

- Under $200 with strong reviews
- Lightweight for stairs
- Good enough for hard floors and low pile
- Removable battery on many SKUs

### Cons

- Hair wrap more frequent than Shark
- Shorter runtime at max power
- Build feels less rugged
